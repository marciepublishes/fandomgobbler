About

About FandomGobbler.

Previously called AO3 Story Tracker.

FandomGobbler started from a desire to keep better track of what I had read on AO3: what I wanted to come back to, what I had finished, what I loved, what disappeared, and what I wanted to remember beyond a browser tab or a bookmark.

From there, it evolved into a broader reading-library tool. AO3 is still the most complete edition, the FanFiction.net version is in beta, and editions like Wattpad and Tumblr are an ambitious goal I have for the future.

The name came from the word gobble (y'know "to eat something quickly and greedily"), which felt pretty accurate for how I was consuming fanfiction. FandomGobbler's name is supposed to reflect that.

Long term, I would like to include other long-form web reading like Substack essays and other serialized work (it would probably be a different extension, but can you imagine if the new one was called "LongGobbler"? oml).

FandomGobbler is an independent project and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or operated by AO3, FanFiction.net, Google, Chrome, Firefox, or their owners.

Dashboard

What the Dashboard Does

The dashboard is the full library workspace. Use it when you want more room than the popup gives you: browsing, filtering, bulk actions, charts, imports, author tools, and category setup all live here.

Browse by status

Jump into All, For Later, Reading, Completed, Re-reading, On Hold, DNF, or Deleted from the left-side snapshot cards and the main library tabs.

Use smart views

Find works that were added recently, completed recently, missing metadata, unrated, subscribed, or still need notes.

Read the charts

See library shape by status, custom category, month added, top fandoms, top authors, top pairings, and personal ratings.

Switch editions

Use the settings menu to switch between AO3 and FanFiction.net libraries. Future editions are listed there as they become available.

Library

Library Management

Search and filters

Search title, author, fandom, relationship, tags, and notes. The filter panel narrows by fandom, author, relationship, rating, metadata, subscription state, and grouping.

Sorts

Sort by recently added, recently updated, popularity, bookmarks, kudos, hits, oldest For Later, longest unread, shortest unread, highest rated, or random.

Bulk actions

Select visible works, open many pages, move them to a status, or untrack them in one pass.

Custom categories

Create custom categories like Tragedy, Spicy, or Bittersweet. You can also hide selected custom category pills from AO3 listing and search pages while keeping the category saved on the work.

Reading

Reading Tools

Floating sidebar

On AO3 pages, the floating button opens your in-page library. You can move it, tuck it away, and reset its position from the popup.

Work-page controls

Track the current work, move it between statuses, add custom categories, save notes, rate it, and record chapter progress from the work page.

Listing badges

On AO3 search and listing pages, tracked works show status badges, category chips, ratings, and chapter progress so you can recognize saved works at a glance.

Themes

Switch AO3 page theme and extension theme separately, or sync them. The dashboard uses its own extension-style theme control.

Tools

Imports and Backups

AO3 bookmarks

Fetch bookmarks from your logged-in AO3 account, review the results, filter candidates, choose a default status, and optionally assign a custom category.

Marked for Later

Import AO3 works from Marked for Later. This is useful when saving on mobile and organizing later on desktop.

Subscription refresh

Refresh tracked AO3 subscription state so the dashboard can show which works are subscribed.

Export and import

Back up your library as CSV or JSON, then import it later if you move browsers, reload the extension, or want a local safety copy.

Sync

Google Sheets Sync

Google Sheets Sync gives your library a backup home in your own Google Drive. It is useful if you read across browsers, move computers, or reinstall the extension.

What gets created

FandomGobbler creates a spreadsheet named FandomGobbler Sync (Do Not Edit This File). It contains raw sync tabs for AO3, FanFiction.net, deletion tracking, and metadata, plus generated viewing tabs named Desktop View and Mobile View.

What you can view

Use Desktop View and Mobile View when you want to browse your library in Google Sheets, especially on mobile. These tabs are rebuilt by the extension, so treat them as view-only.

How not to break it

Do not rename, delete, reorder, hide, or manually edit the raw tabs: _Meta, AO3 Works, FFNet Works, or Deletions. Do not edit header rows. Do not change work IDs, schema/version columns, timestamps, or deletion rows.

If something goes wrong

If the sheet is deleted or permissions change, sync will show an error in the popup. Your local browser library is still the main working copy. Open the popup, use Google Sheets Sync controls, and reconnect or create a new sync sheet from your local data.

Best practice: make changes in FandomGobbler, not directly in the sync spreadsheet. The pretty viewing tabs are safe to read and sort/filter temporarily, but sync may overwrite them the next time it runs.

Alerts

Author Watch

Author Watch tracks specific AO3 authors in specific fandoms. When you refresh, FandomGobbler checks for new matching works and records matches in the popup and dashboard.

Author Watch is AO3-only right now. It is hidden in beta editions that do not support equivalent author/fandom workflows yet.

Platforms

Site Editions

AO3 Edition

The most complete edition. Supports all features: floating sidebar, listing badges, work-page track controls, notes, ratings, chapter progress, bookmark import, Marked for Later import, subscription refresh, Author Watch, relationship tools, engagement-based sorting, and checking for works that may have been deleted.

FanFiction.net Edition (beta)

A separate library bucket with its own tracked works and custom categories. What works now:

  • Track FF.net stories from the story page
  • Quick-add the current story from the popup
  • Floating sidebar on FF.net pages
  • Listing badges on FF.net search and category pages
  • Status organizing, notes, ratings, chapter progress
  • Dashboard edition switching and library management
  • Export and import backup

Not available in the beta:

  • FF.net favorites or reading-list import
  • Author Watch equivalent
  • Subscription or follow checking
  • Popularity or engagement sorting

Coming later

Wattpad and Tumblr are planned for the future, but they are not usable editions yet. They appear in the dashboard switcher as coming-soon options while platform support is still being built.

Storage

Privacy and Storage

FandomGobbler is local-first. Your works, categories, notes, ratings, settings, and tool state are stored in your browser with chrome.storage.local.

If you connect Google Sheets Sync, your library is copied to a spreadsheet in your own Google Drive. That sync file belongs to the Google Account you connect.

Some tools fetch AO3 or FanFiction.net pages only when you ask them to, such as AO3 bookmarks, Marked for Later, subscription refresh, and Author Watch checks.
